The 60-second reply that doubles your bookings

Murtuja Hasan · Founder, Anthropos Automation — reviewed against our client benchmarks
Reviewed 19 Jul 2026 · 7 min read · Applies to solo & small firms

A potential client who contacts a firm is usually contacting two or three. The one that answers first — within minutes, not hours — feels attentive before a word of advice is given. Manual follow-up converts 20–30% of inquiries; a 60-second automated reply plus a follow-up sequence moves you toward 40–60%. [Placeholder content — real copy later.]

What the data says about speed

Someone reaching out is rarely browsing — they have a deadline and a shortlist. The first responder sets the anchor: this one takes me seriously. The uncomfortable part is that none of it requires you to be available — it requires a system that is.

The 60-second reply, step by step

The sequence begins the moment your form is submitted. The first message is not marketing — it is reassurance with a timestamp.

Send an automatic confirmation within 60 seconds: what was received, who will read it, and when they'll hear back. Name a real timeframe you can keep.
Attach trust, not pressure: your credentials and one relevant result — the two things a shortlisting client compares anyway.
If no consultation is booked, send up to three gentle reminders, then stop. Respect converts better than persistence.

What to say (and never say)

The tone decides whether automation feels like care or like a machine. Short sentences, their matter named, no advice in writing at this stage.

No case assessments, fee promises, or confidential details in automated email. The sequence builds the relationship; the advice happens in the consultation — with you.

Common questions

Won't clients notice it's automated?

They notice speed and clarity. The message is honest — it confirms receipt and sets expectations; it doesn't pretend to be you at midnight.

We're a two-person firm. Is this overkill?

The opposite — the smaller the firm, the more it matters, because there is no receptionist catching what you miss. Set up once, runs unattended.

Do I need new software?

Any standard automation platform connected to your form. If we build your system, this sequence is part of the 4-week delivery.
